Answer: Coverage modifications are changes made to insurance policies that include deductibles, policy limits, and coinsurances.

Question: What is a deductible in insurance?
Answer: A deductible is the amount of each claim that the policyholder is responsible for paying before the insurer will pay a claim.

Question: What are the different types of deductibles?
Answer: The different types of deductibles include fixed dollar deductibles, fixed percentage deductibles, disappearing deductibles, franchise deductibles, fixed dollar deductibles per calendar year, and elimination periods.

Question: What is a fixed dollar deductible?
Answer: A fixed dollar deductible has a set dollar amount, regardless of the size of the loss.

Question: What happens if a loss is below the fixed dollar deductible?
Answer: If a loss is below the fixed dollar deductible, the insurer pays nothing for the claim.

Question: What is a fixed percentage deductible?
Answer: A fixed percentage deductible is a deductible that is a percentage of the loss amount.

Question: What are disappearing deductibles?
Answer: Disappearing deductibles are deductibles that decrease over time if no claims are made.

Question: What are franchise deductibles?
Answer: Franchise deductibles are deductibles that apply only if the loss exceeds a certain amount.
